Door of Miyajima

Location: Miyajima, Japan

By Ozaki Yukiko

Budget: 5$

#In Nature

200k likes

The Itsukushima Shrine is the "house" of the famous floating toring. It's a temple by the sea and it is affected by the low and high tide. It's nice, cute, very red... and with quite a few tourists. There's an entrance fee to the Shrine (unlike Torii, which you can enjoy for free).

Grundarfjörður

Location: Grundarfjörður, Iceland

By Evert Elliðason

Budget: Free

#In Nature

#Good for groups

78k likes

During my recent tour of Iceland, I stayed one night at the Grundarfjordur Campground. The campground was clean and well maintained. The location was ideal, as the town had a nice grocery store to stock up on supplies. There is a large swimming pool complex nearby. Kirkjufell Mountain is within a five minute drive and I recommend exploring other areas along the peninsula.

Sydney Opera

Location: Sydney, Australia

By Ethan Hayes

Budget: 45+$

#Urban

#Good for groups

#Needs booking

23k likes

This is THE iconic building of Sydney. It's a landmark that shouldn't be missed, even if you don't see any show inside. Just see the beautiful architecture from outside. We first took a ferry ride & admired the Opera House from afar & then walked right up to it the next day. The history of the House is quite interesting & even if you don't find it out there, do look it up online. We went to a basement sort of area where a beautiful artificial tree had been put up. Tourists are always thronging the place. So be aware of crowds.

Baños de Agua Santa

Location: Baños, Ecuador

By Juanita Agramonte

Budget: Free

#In Nature

#Good for groups

55k likes

Baños de Agua Santa is located in Tungurahua Province and it is found to 45 minutes of Ambato city, this place has a nice weather, also it is one of places most visited for foreigners and citizens of the same country. In Baños de Agua Santa offer its tourists extreme sports such as rafting, climbing, canyoning, swing jump, mountain biking and so on. In addition, the visitors can find a lot of hostels with prices moderate or expensive, a lot of restaurants with local dishes, international gastronomy, but also tourists can eat local dishes or drink delicious fruits smoothies at popular market local.

Le Vieux Port

Location: Marseille, France

By Claudio Giampa

Budget: 20-70$

#Urban

#Good for groups

11k likes

The port is beautiful, the heart of Marseille. In the hot summer, the port comes alive in the evening. Full of people and families going out to eat and drink, watch street musicians. Really lively and fun atmosphere. And beautiful large squares on the south side of the port, just go a little inside the streets and you find them.

Hostaria al gladiatore

Location: Rome, Italy

By Pierre Ouef

Budget: 20-40$

#Family Friendly

#Good for groups

12k likes

This restaurant sits opposite the Colosseum and it truly is a spectacular sight for you to feast your eyes upon when you’re not feasting on their delicious pasta! We took the set menu which contained a glass of wine, bruschetta, and a pasta dish for €15. The carbonara was incredible and we mopped our plates up with bread. Roberto was an excellent host and made our dinner experience even more enjoyable. I look forward to returning when back in Rome!
